1 Planning

The first step in building a garden deck is crucial and requires careful consideration, involves decisions about the deck's size, shape, and style

Planning

Deciding on the size, shape and style of your garden deck is very important regardless of what you intend to use it for.
Do you want the deck to be attached to your house or set back in your garden? Where will it receive the most/least amount of sunshine? Do you want it to be on ground level or raised up? Will it be just a flat deck or do you want balustrades? Do you want built-in seating? The list is endless!

Knowing exactly what you want and where you want it will help you understand the costs and time scales involved.

2 Materials

Selecting the right materials is a crucial decision that can significantly impact the overall look, durability, and maintenance requirements of your deck.

Materials

There are is such a wide variety of woods available that it can be slightly overwhelming!

  • Pine - inexpensive softwood but prone to scratches and dents
  • Fir - inexpensive softwood with a reddish-brown color
  • Spruce - lightweight softwood with a straight grain
  • Cedar - softwood with a natural resistance to water, decay and bugs
  • Redwood - a premium softwood with high resilience when exposed to the elements
  • Walnut - a strong and stable straight-grained hardwood
  • Teak - a hardwood with high durability and water resistance
  • Ipe - a premium-priced exotic hardwood
  • Cherry - a premium-priced hardwood. Unstained it has a rich, beautiful color
  • Oak - a very durable hardwood with a distinctive look
  • Composite - a mixture of wood, plastic and binding agent

3 Measure Up

When planning a new deck, it's essential to physically lay out strings or markers in the actual location to visualize the project's scale effectively.

Measure Up

Measuring out the size of your new deck should not only be done on paper. We recommend laying out some string to the exact positioning of where your deck will be. Seeing it on a piece of paper or on your computer is one thing, but seeing it where it will eventually be will really drive home the scale of the project.

Most people find that seeing it in ‘reality’ makes it look much larger than on paper, so being able to see exactly the scale of the deck is very beneficial.

Once you are happy that the size of deck you want and that it will fit into the space you have, you’re ready to go shopping!

4 Prepare the ground

Is vital to the whole integrity of the structure, which involves clearing the area, checking for proper slope and drainage, and creating a solid foundation.

The ground

Preparing the ground, or ‘oversite’, is vital to the whole integrity of the structure.

  • Clear the Area: Begin by removing any vegetation, rocks, or debris from the designated area where you plan to install the decking. This ensures a clean and level surface.

  • Check for Slope: Verify that the ground has a slight slope away from your home to allow for proper drainage. A gradient of 1/4 inch per foot is typically recommended.

  • Mark the Layout: Mark the layout of your deck using stakes and string lines to define its exact dimensions and shape.

  • Excavate: Dig post holes or trenches for the footings, ensuring they are below the frost line in your area. The depth and spacing will depend on your local building codes.

  • Install Foundation: Pour concrete footings or use pre-made concrete deck blocks as the foundation for your deck's support posts. Make sure they are level and allow for proper support.

  • Add Gravel and Landscape Fabric: Spread a layer of gravel within the excavated area to improve drainage. Lay down landscape fabric to prevent weed growth.

  • Frame the Deck: Construct the frame of the deck using pressure-treated lumber, ensuring it's level and securely anchored to the footings.

  • Attach Joists: Install joists at regular intervals across the frame, providing the necessary support for the decking boards.

  • Decking Material: Finally, lay the decking boards over the joists, leaving appropriate gaps for expansion and drainage. Secure them in place with screws or nails.

  • Finishing Touches: Apply sealant or stain to protect the decking material and enhance its appearance. Add railings, stairs, and any other desired features.

By following these steps, you'll properly prepare the ground for installing decking, ensuring a sturdy and long-lasting outdoor space.
